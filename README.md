## Part I · The Neuroscience of Power and Structural Suppression

**The Mirror Neuron Substrate of the Hubris Singularity**

Hogeveen, Inzlicht & Obhi (2014) establish the precise neural mechanism through which unchecked resource dominance produces the behavioral signature of TJ Detweiler sitting atop worthless stickers: high-power states structurally decrease mirror-neuron system (MNS) activity and measurably reduce motor resonance — the neural substrate of empathy. This is not character failure. It is acquired hardware modification.

The mechanism is dose-dependent and cumulative. Power accumulation is not a switch but a gradient: as unchallenged resource dominance increases, MNS suppression deepens. The predictability horizon of the power-holder's social cognition — their ability to accurately model what their actions mean to others — decays at the Lyapunov rate of their uncontested accumulation:

$$F_{\text{empathy}}(t) \sim F_0 \cdot \exp(-2\lambda_{\text{power}} \cdot t_{\text{uncontested}})$$

Keltner, Gruenfeld & Anderson (2003) document the approach-inhibition mechanism: power activates the behavioral approach system (BAS), increasing reward sensitivity and reducing constraint sensitivity. This produces the exact behavioral signature of the political bypass's final stages — contempt for peer input, messianic certainty, progressive loss of contact with practical consequences — not through willful cruelty but through systematic BAS upregulation that makes social feedback informationally weightless.

**The Neural Correlates of the Competitive Evacuation**

From the outlier's side, sustained social exclusion events — the competitive evacuation arc's serial peer withdrawal — activate identical neural substrates to physical pain. Eisenberger, Lieberman & Williams (2003) demonstrate that dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC) activity during social exclusion (Cyberball paradigm) mirrors its activity during physical pain. The competitive evacuation is not metaphorically painful. It is neurologically indistinguishable from repeated physical injury.

Cumulative dACC activation under chronic social exclusion produces a well-documented secondary effect: hypervigilance to social threat signals. The brain recalibrates its priors toward threat detection. Incoming social data — a pause before responding, a subtle shift in tone, an unexplained absence — is processed through a posterior probability that assigns disproportionate weight to threat interpretations. The sabotage equilibrium *trains* the nervous system to be accurate about social danger in sabotage environments. The problem is that this calibration persists after exit.

This is the neural substrate of the Departure-Confirmation Loop. The loop does not require ongoing sabotage for maintenance. It requires only a nervous system that has been correctly trained to detect the early signatures of sabotage, now operating in environments where those signatures are absent but the detection circuitry remains primed.

**The Cognitive Overhead Tax**

Sweller's cognitive load theory (1988; updated Sweller, van Merriënboer & Paas, 2019) distinguishes intrinsic cognitive load (task complexity), extraneous load (poor instructional design), and germane load (schema construction for deep learning). The sabotage equilibrium imposes massive extraneous cognitive load: constant threat monitoring, political landscape mapping, coalition tracking, reputation management, and counter-bypass vigilance. These operations are not optional; they are correct responses to an informationally threatening environment. They simply compete with germane load — the cognitive work through which capacity is actually developed.

The overhead is not metaphorical. Working memory is finite. Every cycle spent on political surveillance is a cycle not spent on intellectual synthesis. The credential gap widens partly because the outlier's processing capacity is being taxed by structural demands that their peers, below the sabotage-activation threshold, do not face. The gap is produced by the overhead, not by differential capacity.

The Overhead Neutralization that the Exit Theorem achieves is, at the neural level, the recovery of working memory capacity. The political environment stops requiring continuous monitoring. The extraneous load drops. Germane load — the load of genuine intellectual work — becomes the primary cognitive demand for the first time.

## Part II · The Bayesian Brain under Narrative Dispossession

**The Free Energy Principle and the Corrupted Prior**

Friston's free energy principle (2010; extended in Friston et al., 2022) proposes that biological systems — including brains — minimize surprise by maintaining a generative model of their environment and updating it via Bayesian inference. The brain is not a passive receiver of information. It is a prediction machine whose predictions shape what it perceives, and whose generative model is continuously updated by the discrepancy between prediction and sensation.

In this framework, Narrative Dispossession is not a communication problem. It is a **systematic prior corruption event**. The political bypass — through the Credibility Inversion, third-party gaslighting, and authority-figure capture — installs a corrupted generative model of the self's institutional position:

Normal generative model:
$$P(\text{recognition} \mid \text{capacity}) \text{ is positive and informative}$$

Post-Dispossession model:
$$P(\text{recognition} \mid \text{capacity}) \approx P(\text{recognition}) \quad \text{(capacity and recognition decorrelated)}$$

$$P(\text{poor outcome} \mid \text{high performance}) \text{ is elevated by Credibility Inversion evidence}$$

The corrupted model is not irrational. It is the *correct* Bayesian update given the evidence stream the structural environment generates. Every authority-figure feedback filtered through the Credibility Inversion is a genuine data point supporting the corrupted model. Every peer withdrawal produced by the competitive evacuation is genuine evidence the model is correctly updating on. The model is wrong not because the inference is bad but because the evidence is structurally biased.

This is why cognitive reframing without structural change fails. The corrupted prior is being continuously reinforced by ongoing structural evidence. The brain cannot reason its way out of an accurate Bayesian inference. It requires a new evidence stream — or a structural analysis that reveals the evidence as systematically biased, which is the function of the Coherence Reconstruction Protocol.

**The Suppression-Evidence Loop as Bayesian Attractor**

A Bayesian attractor is a prior so strongly reinforced by incoming evidence that it becomes self-sustaining: any new evidence is interpreted through the prior, confirming it, which deepens the prior, which makes further confirmation easier. The Suppression-Evidence Loop is a Bayesian attractor maintained by three simultaneous evidence channels:

1. **The archive channel**: Accumulated autobiographical evidence of suppression, misrecognition, and peer withdrawal — processed through the installed hostile attributional voices that participate in retrieval
2. **The institutional feedback channel**: Real-time authority-figure feedback filtered through the Credibility Inversion

Breaking the attractor requires simultaneously addressing all three channels. The Coherence Reconstruction Protocol addresses the archive channel. The Protocol alone breaks nothing — the Loop has two additional maintenance channels. All three must be addressed, and neither condition alone is sufficient.

**The Default Mode Network as Hostile Territory**

The default mode network (DMN) — medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate cortex, angular gyrus — supports autobiographical memory, future prospection, theory of mind, and narrative self-construction. It is most active when the mind is unconstrained: during rest, daydreaming, and self-reflection.

The Suppression-Evidence Loop runs on DMN substrate. The rumination is not a pathological response; it is the DMN performing its intended autobiographical function on a corrupted dataset.

The psychological experience of the Coherence Reconstruction Protocol — the retrospective reinterpretation of the developmental archive — is phenomenologically identical to what Teasdale et al. The Protocol is not therapy. But it operates on the same neural substrate through the same mechanism: Bayesian prior correction via metacognitive re-processing of archived evidence.

## Part III · The Psychophysiology of the Sabotage Environment

**Polyvagal Theory and the Institutional Nervous System**

Stephen Porges' Polyvagal Theory (2011; revised in Porges & Dana, 2018) identifies three hierarchically organized autonomic circuits, each associated with a distinct behavioral state:

- **Ventral vagal complex (VVC)**: Social engagement, safety, connection, prosocial behavior, open cognition, play
- **Sympathetic nervous system (SNS)**: Mobilization, fight/flight, threat response, narrowed attention, competitive behavior
- **Dorsal vagal complex (DVC)**: Immobilization, shutdown, freeze, dissociation, functional collapse

The *Polyvagal-Institutional Hypothesis* (novel contribution): Institutional allocation mechanisms determine which autonomic circuit is chronically activated at the population level.

Rank-order tournament environments with relative payoff preferences chronically activate SNS circuits through the continuous implicit threat of rank displacement. The peer is not merely a peer; they are a rank competitor whose success is literally a threat to one's institutional standing. This produces the characteristic SNS behavioral signature: hypervigilance, narrowed attention, competitive orientation, reduced prosocial signaling, and the physiological sequelae of sustained cortisol elevation.

Absolute-allocation environments with wide comparison horizons chronically activate VVC circuits — the social engagement system Porges identifies as the evolutionary substrate of mammalian cooperative behavior. When others' success cannot displace yours, the peer triggers VVC activation: social safety, genuine curiosity about their contribution, openness to exchange. This is not a different attitude. It is a different autonomic state, produced by a different institutional architecture.

The behavioral signature of G_coord > 0 environments is ventral vagal: equal conversational participation, social sensitivity to others' contributions (the c-factor predictors Woolley et al. identify), multi-directional knowledge flow. These are not social skills. They are ventral vagal behaviors that emerge automatically when the institutional architecture stops triggering the SNS.

**Allostatic Load and the Physiological Cost of the Equilibrium**

McEwen & Stellar (1993) introduced allostasis — the body's process of achieving stability through change — and allostatic load — the cumulative physiological cost of chronic stress adaptation. The HPA axis, sympathetic-adrenal system, immune function, and hippocampal volume all bear measurable allostatic load from sustained psychosocial stress.

Sustained operation in the sabotage equilibrium produces cumulative allostatic load through three channels:
1. **Chronic social threat activation**: HPA axis dysregulation from repeated social exclusion events
2. **Cognitive overhead depletion**: Prefrontal cortex metabolic cost of sustained political surveillance
3. **Threat-inhibited performance expression**: The physiological toll of systematic suppression of authentic intellectual output

The allostatic load is not recovered by exit alone. McEwen's research establishes that allostatic load accumulation is partially irreversible — hippocampal volume loss from chronic glucocorticoid exposure, immune dysregulation from sustained SNS activation, and HPA axis hyper- or hypo-reactivity from chronic stress do not fully normalize after stress removal. This is the physiological analog of the archive: suppression leaves a biological record as well as a psychological one.

**Social Baseline Theory and the Metabolic Mathematics of Peers**

James Coan's Social Baseline Theory (2008; empirically extended in Coan, Schaefer & Davidson, 2006) proposes that the human brain evolved in a fundamentally social ecology in which other people are expected metabolic resources. The brain's default assumption — encoded in the structure of its regulatory systems — is that managing challenges is a shared enterprise. When others are present and cooperative, the neural cost of coping with threat is distributed; the individual brain can afford to allocate fewer resources to threat management.

The experimental prediction, confirmed: holding a stranger's hand reduces neural threat response (vmPFC, insula) to anticipated shock. Holding a spouse's hand reduces it further. Holding no hand maximizes it.

Applied to the sabotage equilibrium: the environment converts peers from expected metabolic resources into metabolic costs. The presence of coalition members is neurally more threatening than their absence. The social baseline — the evolved expectation of distributed threat management — is systematically violated. The result is a continuous metabolic deficit: the individual brain is spending resources managing social threats that the social environment was supposed to absorb.

The exit decision, in Social Baseline terms, is not a rational calculation. It is the nervous system recognizing that the current social environment is below the metabolic break-even point — costing more resources in social threat management than it returns in social support. The exit is metabolic necessity before it is strategic choice.

**The Landauer Economics of Self**

When TJ sells his red cap for five Mon Stickers, he executes what the ERIE framework names an identity Landauer erasure: erasing an informational dimension of his social self requires entropy production proportional to the information stored in that dimension. The psychology of forced identity liquidation is documented in multiple traditions:
- **Object Relations Theory** (Winnicott, 1953): Transitional objects — objects that carry condensed representations of relational history — cannot be replaced by functional equivalents without psychological cost

The credential gap is a continuous slow-motion version of the red cap sale: the outlier is continuously liquidating the expected future return on capacity investments — the institutional recognition that capacity should generate — at a negative exchange rate. **Attachment Theory and Institutional Belonging**

## Part V · The Phenomenology of Collective Crystallization

**Self-Determination Theory and the Architecture of G_coord**

Deci & Ryan's Self-Determination Theory (2000; extended in Ryan & Deci, 2020) identifies three basic psychological needs whose satisfaction is necessary for intrinsic motivation, wellbeing, and authentic development:
- **Competence**: The experience of effective functioning and mastery
- **Autonomy**: The experience of volition and self-endorsement of one's actions
- **Relatedness**: The experience of close, caring connection with others

The sabotage equilibrium systematically frustrates all three:
- **Competence is corrupted**: The credential gap produces ongoing feedback that performance does not generate recognition, systematically undermining competence experience
- **Autonomy is violated**: Political surveillance, strategic self-concealment, and the expression inversion (performing capacity below its actual level to avoid sabotage activation) produce alienated rather than authentic action
- **Relatedness is inverted**: Peers who should be relatedness resources are converted into competitive threats; connection is dangerous

The Assortment Criterion's five properties jointly satisfy all three needs:
- **Absolute allocation** restores competence feedback — performance again generates proportional recognition
- **Low α + wide comparison horizons** restore autonomy — intellectual output can be authentic rather than strategically managed
- **Conditional cooperator density + wide horizons** restore relatedness — peers become genuine sources of connection rather than rank competitors

G_coord > 0 is the collective-level correlate of simultaneous SDT need satisfaction. It is the mathematical signature of an environment in which all three needs are met for all agents simultaneously. The c-factor that Woolley et al. measure is the aggregate behavioral expression of restored intrinsic motivation.

**Flow and the Phenomenology of Coordination Gain**

Csikszentmihalyi's (1990, 2014) account of flow — optimal experience characterized by effortless attention, intrinsic reward, clear goals, unambiguous feedback, and the experience of stretching capacity while maintaining control — describes the phenomenological signature of G_coord > 0 from the inside.

Flow is most frequently reported in creative, collaborative, and intellectual work at the edge of capacity. It requires:
- Challenge-skill balance: the task is stretching but not overwhelming
- Clear goals and immediate feedback: performance generates unambiguous signal
- Full attentional absorption: no competing cognitive demands drain the resource pool

The sabotage equilibrium destroys all three conditions. The cognitive overhead tax converts intellectual work into multi-threaded threat management. The Credibility Inversion generates ambiguous feedback at best and actively misleading feedback at worst. The political landscape requires constant partial attention, preventing full absorption.

The outlier collective restores all three conditions. The result — and this is the phenomenological prediction — is that high-capacity intellectual work in Assortment-Criterion-satisfying environments is experienced qualitatively differently from the same work in sabotage environments. Not as quantitatively more productive but as intrinsically rewarding in a way that the overhead-taxed version never was. This is the experience of G_coord > 0: not simply better performance metrics but a different phenomenological quality of intellectual engagement.

Csikszentmihalyi's research establishes that flow is the state most associated with subjective wellbeing reports and with the experience of life as meaningful. The outlier collective is not merely more productive than the sabotage equilibrium. It is the environment in which the work feels meaningful for the first time.

**The Broaden-and-Build Dynamics of Collective Coordination**

Fredrickson's Broaden-and-Build Theory (2001; updated in Fredrickson, 2013) establishes that positive emotions — joy, interest, contentment, love — broaden the scope of attention and thought-action repertoires, and that this broadening builds durable psychological resources: intellectual resources (knowledge, cognitive flexibility), social resources (friendship, trust, social capital), and physical resources (health, resilience).

The mechanism is cumulative: positive emotions produce broadened attention, which generates more varied experiences of mastery and connection, which produce more positive emotions. G_coord > 0 environments are positive-emotion generators. The coordination gain is experienced subjectively as the emergence of insights that no individual contribution would have reached — a form of intellectual surprise that consistently generates the positive affect Fredrickson's mechanism requires.

The developmental prediction follows: individuals who have sustained access to G_coord > 0 environments accumulate psychological resources — intellectual flexibility, social trust, resilience — that compound over time through the broaden-and-build mechanism. Individuals in sustained G_coord = 0 (sabotage) environments deplete resources through the narrow-and-deplete mechanism: threat emotions narrow attention, restricted thought-action repertoires generate fewer positive experiences, fewer positive experiences reduce resilience. The developmental bifurcation has a psychological dimension that mirrors and interacts with the institutional one.

## Part VI · The Neuroscience of Collective Intelligence

**The Social Brain and the c-Factor**

Lieberman's (2013) account of the social brain establishes that the neural infrastructure for social cognition — the default mode network, the temporoparietal junction (TPJ), the superior temporal sulcus (STS), the anterior insula — is the same infrastructure that underlies self-referential processing, future planning, and meaning-making. The brain does not treat social cognition as one function among many. It treats it as the substrate on which other cognitive functions rest.

This has a direct implication for the c-factor. The social sensitivity that Woolley et al. identify as the primary predictor of collective intelligence — operationalized via the Reading the Mind in the Eyes test (Baron-Cohen et al., 1997) — is the behavioral expression of intact social brain function. The test measures TPJ-STS-mediated mental state attribution: the ability to accurately model what others are thinking and feeling from minimal cues.

In the sabotage equilibrium, social brain function is operating under the threat-mode suppression documented by Hogeveen et al.: MNS activity is reduced, motor resonance is dampened, and the social cognition infrastructure is partially commandeered for threat processing rather than genuine other-modeling. Social sensitivity declines not because individuals lack the capacity but because the institutional environment produces the neurological conditions under which the capacity is suppressed.

The restoration of social sensitivity in the outlier collective — the recovery of the c-factor predictor — is a neural event: the return of VVC-mediated social engagement allows the social brain infrastructure to operate in its intended mode, generating the genuine other-modeling that equal conversational participation and social sensitivity require.

**Oxytocin, Trust, and the Coordination Threshold**

Kosfeld, Heinrichs, Zak, Fischbacher & Fehr (2005) establish that intranasal oxytocin increases trust in economic games — specifically, the willingness to accept social risk in the trust game — through amygdala downregulation of social threat assessment. Oxytocin does not produce unconditional trust; it recalibrates the risk assessment threshold, making social connection less costly to initiate.

The outlier collective's institutional properties — absolute allocation, wide comparison horizons, high conditional cooperator density — produce the social environment in which endogenous oxytocin release is most likely. Zak, Kurzban & Matzner (2005) establish that oxytocin release is triggered by social signals of trustworthiness and cooperation. When the institutional architecture produces genuine cooperation (because it is individually rational, not because it is normatively enforced), the associated cooperative signals trigger endogenous oxytocin release, which further reduces social threat assessment, which further facilitates cooperation. This is the neurochemical mechanism through which the cooperative equilibrium is self-sustaining.

The sabotage equilibrium produces the opposite: chronic social threat signals suppress endogenous oxytocin release, maintain elevated amygdala reactivity to social stimuli, and sustain the hypervigilance that makes trust costly. The institutions are not merely incentive systems. They are endocrine environments.

The precursor will be distinguishable from general cognitive dissonance by its domain-specificity (targeting institutional feedback rather than other domains) and its predictive relationship to subsequent exit timing.
